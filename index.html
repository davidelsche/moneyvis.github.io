What money can buy

By David Elsche, Sascha Collet

The world is determined by so many little factors that it is impossible to even begin to understand just those that are relevant to ourselves. Big numbers, on the other hand, are often so far beyond the scale of our daily experiences that they become hard to grasp. It is only when those big numbers are put into perspective that we begin to comprehend the sheer power that they possess. Let us have a look at that power.

Scroll

100 $

doesn’t seem like so much. You can get a nice pair of jeans for 100$ or maybe some shoes. But let’s talk about more important things than clothing.

10.000 $

can make a real difference. With that money, aid organizations could buy 33 tons of millet seeds, which can feed a lot of people. Farmers in developing countries often lack the money to raise crops - leading to famine and death which could otherwise be averted. Let’s see what we can do with a bit more money.

100.000 $

will pay for the education of 550 Kenyan children who otherwise would not learn how to read and write. 550 children that could change the future of their families. Imagine what millions could pay for.

8.500.000 $

is the cost of one battle tank. But military equipment gets even more expensive - if you are going for modern warfare.

50.000.000 $

will buy you even more efficient killing machines - 3 Predator drones. Just imagine how much good you could do with that kind of money. But let’s put that into perspective.

25.600.000.000 $

This is the total budget for the U.S. Agency for International Development, an organization within the Department of State, which incidentally amounts to 28% less than the 2017 annualized continuing resolution. Still a lot of money for helping people, to be fair. Let’s see what’s reserved for the military.

>

639.000.000.000 $

Trump’s budget for the Department of Defense is 8% higher compared to 2017. This corresponds to 10 billion less for saving lives and a solid 52 billion more for weapons. But it’s not about the numbers - it’s about what they stand for. Money can make a big difference.
